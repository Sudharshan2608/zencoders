CASE STUDY

AI‑Assisted Triage for Chest Pain in Virtual Care

Researchers: J. Doe (University A), R. Smith (Institute B), M. Lee (Hospital C)

Journal / 2025Clinical AIVirtual Urgent Care

Study Highlights

  • Objective: compare AI guidance vs. final physician recommendations for standardized chest‑pain presentations.
  • Population: U.S. virtual urgent‑care physicians; video vignette cases.
  • Outcome: accuracy and safety signals (red‑flag capture, guideline adherence).

STUDY HIGHLIGHTS PRESENTED BY CO‑AUTHORS: Jane Doe, M.D. and R. Smith, Ph.D.

Comparison of Initial AI and Final Physician Recommendations

AI and Physicians Rated Equal
68%
AI Rated Better
69%
Physicians Rated Better
11%
  • AI advantages: guideline adherence, red‑flag capture, EHR signal use
  • Physician advantages: physical‑exam cross‑references, fewer unnecessary referrals

CASE STUDY

Autonomous Multi‑Agent AI vs. Board‑Certified Clinicians

Researchers: A. Kumar (Tech Univ), S. Nguyen (Health Lab), L. Park (Med Center)

IEEE Conference / 2025Agentic AITelehealth

Study Highlights

  • Objective: benchmark autonomous agentic AI against clinicians on diagnostic and treatment concordance.
  • Population: retrospective sample of real telehealth urgent‑care encounters.
  • Outcome: diagnostic match rate, plan alignment, expert adjudication of discordant cases.

STUDY HIGHLIGHTS PRESENTED BY CO‑AUTHORS: A. Kumar and S. Nguyen

Comparison of AI vs. Physician Outcomes

AI and Physicians Rated Equal
64%
AI Rated Better
24%
Physicians Rated Better
12%
  • AI strengths: documentation consistency, adverse‑event screening, protocolized follow‑ups
  • Clinician strengths: nuanced physical‑sign synthesis, social context integration
